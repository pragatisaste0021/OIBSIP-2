Mahatma Gandhi

Father of our Nation

Timeline of Mahatma Gandhi's Life

  • 1869 : Birth at Porbandar.
  • 1882 : Marriage with Kasturbai, aged 13.
  • 1888 : Son, Harilal born. Reached Southampton, England. Enrolled at the Inner Temple, Inn of Court.
  • 1891 : Called to the Bar.
  • 1892 : Son, Manilal born.
  • 1893 : Reached Durban, Natal. Refused to remove his Paghadi; left the court. Thrown off the first class carriage of the train at Pietermartizburg Station. Read Tolstoy's 'Kingdom of God is Within You'.
  • 1894 : Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'.
  • 1896 : Commenced writing the 'Green Pamphlet' at Rajkot. Public lecture at Poona; chaired by Ramkrishna Bhandarkar.
  • 1897 : Son, Ramdas born.
  • 1899 : Established Indian Ambulance Corp during the Boer War.
  • 1900 : Assisted Kasturbai in the delivary of son Devadas.
  • 1903 : First issue of 'Indian Opinion' published.
  • 1904 : Established 'Phoenix Settlement'.
  • 1906 : Took the vow of Brahmcharya. Advent of Satyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, where people took a pledge with God as their witness, to oppose the Black Act. Left for England as a member of the Indian Delegation.
  • 1907 : Asiatic Registration Bill passed in Transvaal Parliament.
  • 1908 : The term 'Satyagraha' adopted based on Maganlal Gandhi's fomulation 'Sadagraha'. Awarded two months' simple imprisonment for refusal to obtain registration. Agreement signed with General Smuts on voluntary registration. Mir Alam and others assault Gandhiji. Breach of faith and bonfire of certificate. Awarded two months' rigourous imprisonment for entry into the Transvaal without permit.
  • 1909 : Left for England as a member of the Indian Delegation. Wrote 'Hind Swarajya' aboard the ship 'Kildonan Castle'. 'Hind Swarajya' published in 'Indian Opinion'.
  • 1910 : 'Hind Swarajya' proscribed. Sent a copy of English rendering 'Indian Home Rule' to Tolstoy. Established Tolstoy Farm; vowed to eschew milk; commenced experiments in frutarian diet.
  • 1912 : Gopalkrishna Gokhale's South Africa tour commenced.
  • 1913 : Kasturba and others arrested. Kasturba was awarded 3 months' rigourous imprisonment. MKG released from jail unconditionally with others, before the completion of the period of imprisonement. Kasturba released from prison.
  • 23-Sep 1913 :Kasturba was awarded 3 months' rigourous imprisonment.
  • 26 June 1914 : Indians' Relief Bill (1914), passed. Travelled to London to meet Gokhale; Phoenix party at Santiniketan.
  • 1915 : Visited Santiniketan. Visited Kumbh Mela, Hardwar. Established 'Satyagraha Ashram', Kochrab. Awarded the 'Kaisare Hind' medal. First Harijan family of Dudabhai, Danibehn and Laxmi joined the Ashram. Elected as Vice President of Gujarat Sabha.
  • 1916 : Lectured at Benaras Hindu University. Met Jawaharlal Nehru for the first time at Lucknow Congress.
  • 1917 : Travelled to Patna with Rajkumar Shukla to investigate condition of indigo farmers. Gave a statement in the court about disobedience of prohibitary orders. Sabarmati Ashram established. Presided over the Second Gujarati Education Conference, Bharuch. Presided over the First Gujarat Political Conference, Godhara. Mahadev Desai joined as Secretary.
  • 1918 : Appointed to arbitar the dispute between Mill-owners and Mill-hands of Ahmedabad. Indefinite fast to keep the resolve of striking Mill-hands commenced. Broke fast after compromise. Addressed the public meeting of 5000 peasants at Nadiad; advised them to not pay land revenue.
  • 1919 : Satyagraha pledge against 'Rowlett Act'. National Hartal and Satyagraha against the Rowlett Act. Arrested at Palval Station. First issue of 'Navajivan' published. First issue of 'Young India' published. Congress boycott the Hunter Commission and set up independent inquiry.
  • 1920 : Returned the honours to the Government, commencement of Non-co-operation. Gujarat Vidyapith established.
  • 1921 : Given the reigns of the Congress in Ahmedabad.
  • 1922 : Bardoli Taluka resolved against payment of land revenue and Civil-Disobedience. Policemen at Chauri Chaura killed. Gandhiji and Shankerlal Banker arrested. Charged of sedition for the authorship of three articles in Young India. Trial held at Ahmedabad Circuit House; Judge Broomfield awarded Gandhiji a sentence of six years' imprisonment. Transferred to Yeravda Prison.
  • 1924 : Operated for appendicites at Sasoon Hospital, Poona. Released unconditionally. The publication of 'Dakshin Africana Satyagrahano Itihas' in Navajivan. Twenty one days' fast for communal harmony commenced at Maulana Mohammed Ali's house, Delhi. Presided over the Belgaum Congress.
  • 1925 : 'All India Spinners' Association' established. Madeleine Slade joined Satyagraha Ashram, named Mirabehn. The publication of 'Satyana Prayogo athava Atmakatha' commences in Navajivan. The publication of 'Autobiography or The Story of My Experiments with Truth' commences in Young India.
  • 1928 : Boycott of Simon Commission.
  • 1929 : Wrote introduction to 'Anasktiyoga'. Viceroy Lord Irwin annouces Round Table Conference. Purna Swaraj Resolution adopted at Lahore Congress.
  • 1930 : Wrote to the Viceroy about his intention to break the Salt Law. Dandi March commences from Satyagraha Ashram. Broke the Salt Law. Arrested and sent to Yeravda Prison.
  • 1931 : Released from prison. Gandhi-Irwin pact signed. In London as the sole representative of the Congress at the Second Round Table Conference. Recorded a message for the people of America. Met Maria Montessori. 'God is' recorded. Attended (in his loin-cloth) the Royal reception for the delegates of the Second Round Table Conference at the Buckingham Palace at the invitation of Emperor George V. Declared his opposition to the proposed Communal Award. Second Round Table Conference ends. With Romain Rolland. In Vatican. Met Mussolini in Rome.
  • 1932 : Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution of Civil Disobedience. Arrested and sent to Yeravda for indefinite period; Vallabhbhai fellow prisoner. Mahadev Desai transfered to Yeravda. Premier Ramsay Macdonald announced the Communal Award. Letter to Ramsay Macdonald declaring his intentions to fast unto death against the Communal Award. The fast commenced. Poona Pact signed. Broke the fast. 'Harijan Sevak Sangh' established.
  • 1933 : Commneced publication of 'Harijan'. 'Harijan Sevak' (Hindi), commenced publication on 23rd February and 'Harijanbandhu' (Gujarati) commenced publication on 12th March. Resolved to fast for 21 days at midnight. Issued a statement about the fast; unconditional and for self-purification. Commenced the fast. Released from prison. Fast broken. Declare his intention to offer Individual Satyagraha. Arrested at Ahmedabad and sent to Sabarmati Prison; shifted to Yeravda later. Went to Wardha with a decision to not involve himself with politics. Satyagraha Ashram, Sabarmati given to Harijan Sevak Sangh. Nationwide Harijan Yatra commenced.
  • 1934 : A mob attacked in Bihar at the instigation of Lalanath Shastri. Toured Orissa on foot. Withdrew Collective Satyagraha. Bomb thrown at his motorcade in Poona. Declared his intention to retire from Congress. Resigned from the Congress. 'All India Village Industries' Association' established.
  • 1936 : Moved to Segaon from Wardha. Presided over the 12th Gujarati Sahitya Parishad. Travancore temples opened to Harijans.
  • 1939 : Kasturba arrested in Rajkot by Thakoresaheb. Commenced fast at Rajkot against the breach of trust. Commenced fast at Rajkot against the breach of trust. Broke the fast; Chief Justice Maurice Gwyer appointed the arbitrator. Sir Maurice Gwyer declared award in favour of the people of Rajkot; Muslims and Bhayats demonstrate against Gandhiji and disrupt a prayer meeting. Wrote a letter to Adolf Hitler. Congress Ministries resign.
  • 1940 Segaon named Sevagram. Congress Working Committee meeting at Sevagram; Individual Satyagraha proposed. Vinoba chosen as the first Individual Satyagrahi.
  • 1941 : 'On Constructive Programme' published. The Congress Working Committee at Bardoli accepted Gandhiji's request to free him from the responsibility of guiding the Congress.
  • 1942 : Informed Congress Working Committee at Sevagram that Jawaharlal Nehru would be his political heir. Advised the Cripps Mission to return to England. Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution for immediate withdrawl of the British from India. 'Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'. Gandhiji and Congress Working Committee members arrested; Gandhiji imprisoned at the Aga Khan Palace. Death of Mahadev Desai;cremated at Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1942 : 'Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'.
  • 1942 : Gandhiji and Congress Working Committee members arrested; Gandhiji imprisoned at the Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1942 : Death of Mahadev Desai;cremated at Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1943 : Commenced a 21 day fast.
  • 1944 : Death of Kasturba; cremated at Aga Khan Palace. Released unconditionally.
  • 1945 : Lord Wavell calls for negotiations. Congress Working Committee members released from Ahmedanagar Fort Prison. Ban on Congress lifted. The Viceroy declared the Simla Conference a failure.
  • 1946 : Three member British delegation reaches Delhi. The Congress Working Committee adopted resolution to accept the formation of the Constituent Assembly. The Viceroy formed an Interim Government. Communal riots in Calcutta. A 12 member Interim Government headed by Pandit Nehru formed. Communal violence in Noakhali. Muslim League representatives joined the Interim Government. Communal riots in Bihar. Left for Noakhali. Handed over the responsibility of Harijan Journals to Kaka Kalelkar, Kishorelal Mashruwala and Narahari Parikh.
  • 1947 : Bare Foot March to Noakhali commences. Tour of violence-affected villages of Bihar. Met the new Viceroy Lord Mountbatten. Addressed the Asian Relations Conference. Left for Bihar. The Congress, the Muslim League and the representatives of the Sikhs accept the plan of Partition. The Congress Working Committee adopted resolution to accept the Partition. In Calcutta with Shaheed Suhrawardy spent the day in fast and prayer. Indefinite fast against the communal violence in Calcutta. Broke the fast. Reached Delhi.
  • 1948 : Commenced fast at Delhi. Broke the fast. A bomb thrown during the Prayer Meeting. Took three bullets on his chest. Godse the assassin.Cremated on the banks of the Yamuna by Ramdas.

Biography

Mahatma Gandhi, byname of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, (born October 2, 1869, Porbandar, Indiaâ€”died January 30, 1948, Delhi), Indian lawyer, politician, social activist, and writer who became the leader of the nationalist movement against the British rule of India. As such, he came to be considered the father of his country. Gandhi is internationally esteemed for his doctrine of nonviolent protest (satyagraha) to achieve political and social progress.
